The High Performance Geophysics Lab, or HPG, is a multidisciplinary research laboratory that aims at solving geophysics related problems by combining expertises in geophysics, geology, physics, applied math and computer science. The lab has successfully developed research projects with support from major industry players, such as Petrobras, Baker Hughes, ANP, NVidia, Microsoft and AMD.

The lab is coordinated by Prof. Martin Tygel (CEPETRO/Unicamp), and Prof. Edson Borin (Institute of Computing/Unicamp). The HPG team is composed by roughly 24 people, including geophysics and computer science researchers, professional geophysicists and software developers, IT and support staff, PhD and Ms.C. students, and undergraduate research students.

The HPG lab is located inside the Center for Petroleum Studies (CEPETRO), at the University of Campinas (Unicamp), and its facilities include a high-end cluster for high-performance computing and offices for researchers working on its projects.

State-of-the-art Geophysics

At the HPG, we develop state-of-the-art geophysics methods to improve seismic imaging.

State-of-the-art HW and SW technology

At the HPG, we constantly research the use of new hardware and software technology to accelerate our software tools. Hardware accelerators such as GPUs and the Intel Xeon PHI are examples of such technologies

Reliable and effective HPC

At the HPG, we have developed computing technologies that enable our tools to run on systems composed by multiple computing clusters at the same time. It also tolerates node failures and leverages a dynamic provisioning mechanism to allow scaling-up or down during processing.

Effective Technology Transfer

At the HPG, we are proud to deliver our partners with software tools that enable them to processes their datasets on their in-house HPC clusters.
Our team thrives at transferring technology by installing and testing our software tools at the partners premises and by supporting them with manuals and technical visits.
